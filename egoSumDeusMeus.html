Ego Sum Deus Meus

/2023

GOD IS DEAD. GOD REMAINS DEAD. AND WE HAVE KILLED HIM.

——Friedrich Nietzsche

/The Project

A COMPLEX JOURNEY INTO SIMPLICITY. A LITTLE PERSONAL REFLECTION ON PHOTOGRAPHY SUPPORTED BY A COLLECTION OF IMAGES RECOVERED AND REVALUED THROUGH CUTTINGS AND ENLARGEMENTS.

1

The idea of God's demise prompts us to shape our own divinity. Take a step closer to my true self, embrace it, accept it, and genuinely love it. The path unfolds with subtle influences—moving from powerless prayers to gradually discovering my inner strength, experiencing a true rise, getting closer to my essence, and wholeheartedly embracing all responsibilities.

2

Voidness(Śūnyatā) is everything, everything is voidness. Embracing the ethereal means embracing everything and the world. I don’t need to open my mouth, I use pure body parts to touch the air and all living things.

3

Imogen Cunningham photographs all kinds of flowers, while Geogria Okeeffe magnifies tiny blooms into something that is impossible to ignore. Standing in the middle of the flowers, it feels like being warmly accepted by other women. They hug themselves with their own hands, hug them hard, and convey every inch of love and warmth to themselves.

4

Color holds a profound symbolism: green embodies life, pink exudes tenderness, and orange radiates passion. Delve into the diverse spectrum of colors to unearth facets of your true self, each hue a unique mirror reflecting the depths of your being.

5

In Louise Bourgeois's realm, spiders cease to be daunting. Their delicate legs extend like a mother's warm, sturdy embrace, shielding the offspring beneath. Within the spider's embrace, protected and secure, self-embrace becomes a communion of spirit.
